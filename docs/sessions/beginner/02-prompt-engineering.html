Prompt Engineering Foundations

The difference between a bad prompt and a great one is the difference between garbage output and magic. Learn the craft.

60 min Beginner 3 Labs 5 Quiz Questions

Why Prompting Is a Skill, Not a Trick

A prompt is your interface to an LLM. The model doesn't know what you want — it only knows what you say. Prompting is the art of communicating intent clearly enough that the model's output matches your actual goal.

This isn't about magic words or hacks. It's about understanding how LLMs work and structuring your request to exploit their strengths while avoiding their weaknesses.

💡

The Core Insight

LLMs are next-token predictors trained on human writing. The best prompt is the one that makes the correct output the most statistically likely continuation of your input.

The 5 Elements of a Great Prompt

Every effective prompt has up to five components. You don't always need all five — but knowing them lets you diagnose why a prompt fails.

🎭

1. Role

Tell the model who it is. Sets the persona, expertise level, and tone for the response.

"You are a senior data scientist..."

📋

2. Instruction

What you want the model to DO. Be specific about the task, action, and desired format.

"Summarize the following in 3 bullet points..."

📄

3. Context

Background information the model needs. Your audience, the document, the goal, the constraints.

"The audience is non-technical executives..."

✏️

4. Examples

Show the model what good output looks like. One example often beats 100 words of description.

"Example output: [your sample here]"

🚧

5. Constraints

What the model should NOT do. Length limits, forbidden topics, required formats.

"Do not include jargon. Max 200 words."

Bad Prompt vs Good Prompt: Side-by-Side

Let's see these elements in action with a real example: asking an LLM to write a professional email.

❌ Bad Prompt
Write an email about the meeting.
✅ Good Prompt
You are an executive assistant at a SaaS company.

Write a concise meeting follow-up email to the product team.

Context: We met today to discuss Q3 roadmap priorities. Three items were agreed: (1) launch mobile app by Sep 1, (2) deprecate legacy API, (3) hire 2 ML engineers.

Keep it under 150 words. Professional but warm tone. No jargon.
⚠️

Why Vague Prompts Fail

The model has no idea which meeting, who the recipients are, what tone is appropriate, or what format you want. It will produce something generic. The output quality is a direct reflection of the prompt quality.

Advanced Prompting Techniques

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

LLMs produce better answers to complex problems when they "think aloud" step-by-step. Adding "think step by step" or showing reasoning in your examples dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and multi-step tasks.

❌ Without CoT:
Q: A store has 50 apples. It sells 23. Then gets a shipment of 40 more.
   How many apples does it have?
A: 67

✅ With Chain-of-Thought:
Q: A store has 50 apples. It sells 23. Then gets a shipment of 40 more.
   How many apples does it have? Think step by step.
A:
Step 1: Start with 50 apples.
Step 2: Sell 23 → 50 - 23 = 27 apples.
Step 3: Receive 40 more → 27 + 40 = 67 apples.
Answer: 67 apples.

Few-Shot Prompting

Show the model 2–5 examples of input → output pairs before your actual request. This "programs" the model's behavior without fine-tuning.

prompt = """
Classify the sentiment of product reviews as POSITIVE, NEGATIVE, or NEUTRAL.

Review: "Battery lasts forever, best phone I've owned!"
Sentiment: POSITIVE

Review: "Arrived broken, terrible packaging."
Sentiment: NEGATIVE

Review: "It's okay. Does what it says."
Sentiment: NEUTRAL

Review: "Fast delivery but the color looks different from photos."
Sentiment: ???
"""
# The model will output: NEGATIVE (because the complaint outweighs the positive)

System Prompts

Most LLM APIs have a "system" message separate from the user message. This sets persistent behavior, persona, and constraints for the entire conversation.

import openai

client = openai.OpenAI(api_key="your-key")

response = client.chat.completions.create(
    model="gpt-4o",
    messages=[
        {
            "role": "system",
            "content": """You are TechBot, a customer support assistant for Acme Software.
Rules:
- Only answer questions about Acme products
- If asked about competitors, politely decline
- Always end responses with 'How else can I help?'
- Keep answers under 100 words"""
        },
        {
            "role": "user",
            "content": "How do I reset my password?"
        }
    ]
)
print(response.choices[0].message.content)

Why Prompts Fail: Diagnosis Guide

SymptomRoot CauseFix
Output is too genericNo context or examples providedAdd context about audience, purpose, constraints
Wrong format (prose when you wanted bullet points)Format not specifiedExplicitly state: "Respond with a numbered list"
Too long / too shortNo length constraintAdd: "in exactly 3 sentences" or "max 200 words"
Hallucinated factsModel filling gaps with guessesAdd "Only use information from the provided text" + provide the text
Wrong expertise levelNo role or audience definedSet role: "Explain like I'm 12" or "Assume expert audience"
Off-topic responseAmbiguous instructionBe more specific; add constraints on what NOT to do

Quick Check

❓ A prompt says: "Summarize this article." What's the most important missing element?▼
The article itself (context) — and also: who is the summary for? How long? What format? Without these, the model will produce a generic paragraph-length summary with no particular audience in mind.
❓ You want an LLM to always respond in Spanish. Where should you put that instruction?▼
The System Prompt — System prompts persist across all messages in a conversation. Putting it in the user message means you'd have to repeat it every turn.

Task 1: Email Writing

1

Write a Bad Prompt First

Start with: Write a professional email about a project delay. Copy the output. Notice how vague it is.

2

Upgrade to a Full 5-Element Prompt

You are a senior project manager at a software consultancy.

Write a professional email to our client (Acme Corp) notifying them
that the mobile app launch is delayed by 2 weeks due to unexpected
security vulnerabilities discovered during QA testing.

The email should:
- Apologize sincerely but not excessively
- Briefly explain the reason (security issues found in auth flow)
- Provide the new launch date (September 15th instead of September 1st)
- Offer a call this week to discuss concerns
- End on a positive, forward-looking note

Tone: Professional but warm. Max 200 words. No bullet points in the email itself.

Run this and compare the output to the bad prompt. Notice the difference in specificity, tone, and usefulness.
